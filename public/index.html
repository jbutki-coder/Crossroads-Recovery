House Rules & Program Info

Crossroads Recovery Community provides structured, peer-supported recovery housing focused on accountability, stability, and long-term recovery. This page summarizes the resident intake packet, house rules, and program expectations.

Welcome to Crossroads

Welcome to Crossroads Recovery Community. We are glad you are here. At Crossroads, we come from a place of love and grace. Many of us have stood where you are standing now. Our role is not simply to tell you what to do, but to walk this road of recovery alongside you.

You are not alone here. You are capable of change, and your recovery matters.

Recovery Starts Here.

Our Promise to You

At Crossroads Recovery Community, we promise to lead with love, grace, and honesty. We commit to walking this recovery journey alongside you, not above you.

  • Provide a safe, structured recovery environment
  • Lead with consistency, fairness, and transparency
  • Hold you accountable with compassion and clarity
  • Support your growth while encouraging independence
  • Respect your voice, your effort, and your recovery process

Mission, Vision, and Values

Mission: To provide safe, structured recovery housing that supports personal accountability, peer connection, and long-term recovery.

Vision: A community where individuals in recovery rebuild their lives with dignity, stability, and purpose.

Core Values: Accountability, Respect, Honesty, Consistency, Peer Support, Growth.

Program Overview

Crossroads Recovery Community is a structured recovery housing program operating under peer-supported principles. Each home houses approximately 5-9 residents and follows consistent guidelines and expectations.

The program supports residents in maintaining sobriety, establishing structure and routine, building life skills, strengthening recovery supports, and preparing for independent living.

1. Sobriety

  • Residents must remain alcohol- and drug-free at all times.
  • No possessing, using, selling, or bringing mind- or mood-altering substances into the house.
  • Random drug and alcohol testing may be conducted at any time.

2. Daytime Expectations / Employment

  • Until employed, residents are required to be in the office from 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM unless attending treatment, school, or another approved activity.
  • Residents are expected to actively seek employment, education, or volunteer opportunities if not already working or in programming.
  • Once employed, residents must provide a work schedule when requested.
  • Sleeping, resting, watching TV, loitering, panhandling, unapproved travel, or spending extended time at community spaces without a recovery or employment purpose do not meet the requirement.
  • Approved meetings or appointments are limited to the duration of the meeting or appointment.

3. SSI / Disability Daytime Expectations

Residents with documented disability status may be approved for modified daytime expectations. These modifications do not remove accountability, but allow for reasonable accommodations consistent with recovery goals.

  • Medical, mental health, or therapy appointments
  • Approved recovery programming or peer-support groups
  • Independent life-skills development
  • Scheduled volunteering or approved community engagement
  • Educational classes, skill-building workshops, or vocational exploration
  • Rest periods as medically recommended and approved

Documentation of SSI/SSDI or medical verification must be on file.

4. Visitors

  • No visitors are allowed inside the house.
  • No visitors are allowed in bedrooms at any time.
  • Residents may not bring or allow visitors who are under the influence.
  • Outside visitation concerns will be addressed by management case by case.

5. Vehicle Rule

  • No vehicle is allowed for the first 60 days.
  • After 60 days, any vehicle must be approved by management.
  • Residents must provide a valid driver’s license, registration, and proof of insurance before a vehicle is permitted.

6. Chain of Command

If a resident has a problem, concern, request, or conflict, the chain of command is:

House Manager → Jackson and/or Peer Coach → Angela → Sean

Residents are expected to follow this chain of command unless there is an emergency or immediate safety issue.

7. Meetings, Sponsorship, and Service

  • Residents in all phases are required to attend recovery meetings.
  • Residents are required to obtain and work with a sponsor or mentor.
  • Residents are expected to be of service to the recovery fellowship they belong to.
  • House meetings are mandatory.
  • Daily morning meeting/meditation is required unless excused for work, treatment, or another approved reason.

8. SSD Community Service Requirement

  • Any resident receiving SSD is required to participate in 20 hours of community service.
  • A list of approved community service activities will be provided.
  • Approved service locations may include soup kitchens, homeless shelters, Salvation Army, BWROC, Dry Dock Recovery Center, and other approved sites.

9. Curfew

  • Curfew is 9:30 PM for the first 30 days.
  • After that, curfew is 11:00 PM unless otherwise directed by staff.

10. Sign In / Sign Out

  • Residents must sign in and out whenever entering or leaving the house.
  • Staff should know each resident’s schedule and where they are expected to be.

11. Respect and Conduct

  • No violence, threats, intimidation, harassment, or disruptive behavior.
  • No sexual harassment, hateful language, racial slurs, or discriminatory comments.
  • Residents are expected to treat staff, housemates, neighbors, and guests with respect at all times.

12. House Cleanliness

  • Residents must keep bedrooms, bathrooms, and common areas clean.
  • Chores are assigned and must be completed daily.
  • No food or drinks are allowed in bedrooms.
  • Residents must clean up after themselves in the kitchen, bathrooms, and laundry area.

13. Bedrooms and Personal Space

  • Residents must sleep in their assigned bed.
  • Residents are not allowed in another resident’s room.
  • Personal belongings and medications must be stored properly and kept secure.

14. Smoking and Safety

  • No smoking inside the house.
  • Smoking is only permitted in designated areas.
  • No candles, incense, or other fire hazards are allowed.
  • Staff may conduct room and property checks to maintain safety and accountability.

15. Legal and Behavioral Expectations

  • Residents must follow all laws and probation/parole requirements.
  • Theft, gambling, borrowing without permission, or criminal behavior may result in discharge.
  • Damage to house property may result in financial responsibility and possible termination.

16. General Expectations

  • Residents are expected to contribute to a healthy, respectful, recovery-focused environment.
  • Failure to follow house rules may result in consequences up to and including discharge.

17. Medications Policy

  • Residents must disclose all prescribed medications upon intake.
  • Residents must take medications only as prescribed.
  • Residents must secure medications as directed.
  • Residents must not share medications with others.
  • Misuse or diversion of medication may result in disciplinary action.

18. Financial Responsibilities

  • Residents are responsible for weekly program fees as outlined upon intake.

Resident Acknowledgment

Residents acknowledge that the program is built on structure, accountability, peer support, and mutual respect. Residents agree to participate fully, remain abstinent from alcohol and non-prescribed substances, respect staff and peers, accept accountability and redirection, and communicate concerns appropriately and respectfully.

About This App

What this app is for

This app gives Crossroads residents quick access to recovery help, meeting finders, literature, recovery planning, job leads, house rules, document help, transportation information, and leadership contact options.

Emergency disclaimer

This app is not an emergency service. If someone is overdosing, violent, suicidal, seriously injured, or in immediate danger, call 911.

Anonymous concerns

Anonymous concerns are reviewed by leadership. If no name or contact information is provided, leadership may not be able to follow up directly. False reports can harm the recovery community.

Relapse help requests

Submitting a relapse help request does not guarantee an immediate response. Residents should also call support numbers directly when immediate help is needed.
